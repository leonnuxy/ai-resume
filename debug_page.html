Machinery Asset Specialist

Apply now »

Date: Feb 20, 2025

Location: Calgary, AB, CA, T2A 7V2 Edmonton, AB, CA, T6E 5L9 Grande Prairie, AB, CA, T8V 6M8 Fort St. John, British Columbia, CA, V1J 6P3 Red Deer, AB, CA, T4P 3R3

Company: Spartan Controls Ltd.

Spartan Controls is an employee-owned company and leading provider of data management, industrial automation, valves, measurement and control solutions for process industries in Western Canada and beyond.  We are dedicated to providing exceptional sales, support and service experiences for our diverse customer base in industries such as mining, power, oil & gas, municipal, pulp & paper, and chemical.

 

Spartan is committed to creating a sustainable modern world with innovative automation. Our focus is Customer Outcomes.  By leveraging applied automation we assist customers improving their Safety, Reliability, Operations and Productivity.  

 

Our employee ownership model creates a unique culture of community, creativity, entrepreneurship, and a place to build your career.  Spartan Controls provides an environment that not only encourages you to do your best, but also empowers you to do what it takes to create a solution, address a need, or respond to an issue.

 

Summary

We are currently recruiting for Machinery Asset Specialists to join Spartan’s Reliability Services Team, based in the Calgary, Red Deer, Edmonton, and/or Peace River regions (Grande Prairie or Fort St. John).  The successful candidate will work directly at customer sites and/or remotely at the Spartan Controls office or your home-based office. You will support the delivery of machinery condition monitoring and diagnostic services, as well as consult and implement advanced maintenance strategies to new and existing clients. You will report directly to the Manager, Rotating Equipment & Reliability Engineering.

Role and Responsibilities

A major focus will be on Vibration Analysis of rotating and reciprocating machinery but will also include design, implementation and support of reliability programs. These programs may include utilization of Vibration, Oil, Infrared, Ultrasonic and Motor technologies.

Design:

  • Technology selection/optimization for critical and essential assets
  • Plant control system and machine condition monitoring
  • Program integration
  • Support design and development of program goals and KPI's

Implementation:

  • System/program installation and commissioning
  • Technology deployment, baseline acquisition

Support:

  • Field data capture, analysis and reporting
  • Asset and system optimization
  • Formal and informal training
  • Data Analytic Software Tools

Qualifications and Skills

  • Engineering Degree (Mechanical preferred) or Engineering Technologist Diploma, or a Trade Certification, (Heavy Duty, Automotive, Millwright)
  • Knowledge and experience with rotating & reciprocating machinery would be beneficial
  • Strong mechanical aptitude, as well as product and application understanding 
  • Formal Vibration, Thermography, and Tribology training would be beneficial 
  • Proven customer service skills
  • Effective interpersonal, communication and organizational skills 
  • Base knowledge of Network and IT systems
  • Highly motivated and innovative self-starter
  • Ability to work in a team environment 
  • Valid driver’s license required
  • Entrepreneurial work ethic

Spartan recognizes that there are many ways in which candidates develop knowledge, abilities, and competencies throughout their careers. We encourage applications from candidates with a variety of backgrounds and we do consider qualifications and competencies that are equivalent to those specifically mentioned above.

 

Spartan is committed to providing a safe workplace for all employees. This position is classified as safety-sensitive, and as such, the successful candidate will be required to pass a pre-employment alcohol & drug test.
